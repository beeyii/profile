I've sailed through Three out of the Seven Seas.

Brian Yi


Branch: United States Navy


Rate: Logistics Specialist 2nd Class


Served: 08/27/2013~08/26/2017

Anchors away!

If I were to tell you about my whole Navy experience I think this page would turn into a novel, so let me tell you briefly about what I did in the Navy.

On August 27, 2013 I began bootcamp in Great Lakes, Illinois. In bootcamp I was took on a leadership position in my division. As the administrative leader and I organized everyone's file and paperwork, and planned schedule so that they were ready to be presented when the executive leaders asked about each individual's report. Eight weeks later, I completed my basic training and I was sent to Meridian, Mississippi to complete my Logistics Specialists School. While at the Logistics Specialists School studied supply chain management, accounting, finance, and operations. The software I used in the Navy was called Naval Aviation Logistics Command Management Information System (NALCOMIS). Through NALCOMIS we purchased materials, parts, supplies, etc. we built requisitions; performed ad-hoc analysis; pooled data; planned servicing and organized maintenance schedule.

On February 19, 2014 I landed in Japan and reported to Atsugi, Japan to perform my duties as a Logistics Specialist. I was extremely nervous and didn't know what to expect nor what was going to be expected of me. I was as nervous as anyone on their first job after college. Even though I learned how to be a Logistics Specialist and how to procure items and how to perform ad-hoc queries, but what if I don't have the right answer?
